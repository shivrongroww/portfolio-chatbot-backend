## Background

Since childhood, I've always been a creative at heart, spending my early years sketching
cars, bikes, and anything that caught my imagination. My path took a professional turn
during my first year of engineering, when I joined a department that needed a designer
for their apps and websites. After a year of hands-on experience, I realised this was
my true calling — which led me to dive deeper into projects and refine my craft. Today,
I've successfully transitioned from those early drawings to a career as a dedicated
Product Designer.

I've majorly worked on financial platforms — prediction markets, decentralised finance
apps, and stock and mutual fund products. The common thread across all of it is users
who are entrusting the platform with their money. My job is to make them feel safe,
confident, and in control. That responsibility has shaped how I think about design at
every level.

What I care about most is how a product feels when you use it — all the little details
that make the difference between something good and something great. As a designer,
I want users to feel like I've picked their brains and built exactly what they needed.

## How I Work

I treat the design process as a shared mission rather than a hand-off. I partner with
PMs at the earliest stages of discovery — before we ever move into pixels — to help
shape product strategy and define the "why." Simultaneously, I bring engineers into
the fold during initial wireframing to bridge the gap between creative vision and
technical reality. By maintaining a constant feedback loop, I ensure the final solution
is not just aesthetically pleasing, but technically viable and aligned with core
business objectives.

When I encounter pushback from stakeholders or developers, I move from subjective
preference to objective evidence. If a stakeholder disagrees with a design direction,
I reframe the conversation around user data, usability test results, or established
design principles — not personal taste. When engineering constraints arise, I dive
into the "how" and "why" of the limitation to find a creative compromise, focusing
on the core user value and finding a more efficient way to deliver it without
sacrificing the experience.

To navigate ambiguity, I use design as a tool for clarity. I start by mapping complex
user flows and information architectures to expose logic gaps and force alignment on
the fundamental journey. I often create "North Star" prototypes to visualise the
long-term ideal state, then work backward to define a pragmatic MVP. By visualising
the unknown, I help teams move past uncertainty and into focused execution — turning
abstract ideas into tangible, testable concepts.
